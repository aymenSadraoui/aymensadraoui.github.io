My work focuses on developing AI-based diagnostic and prognostic models for Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC) from histopathological whole slide images.
On the diagnostic side, I designed deep learning frameworks for the automated detection and classification of tumor architectures, leveraging fully-supervised models at different scales, few-shot models, and transductive learning to handle limited labeled data and improve spatial coherence.
For the prognostic aspect, my work aims to predict recurrence risk by integrating various AI-based features extracted from WSIs with clinical and macroscopic data. This involves building interpretable models linking visual phenotypes to patient outcomes.
In parallel, I explore convex optimization and algorithm unrolling techniques for stain separation and normalization in histopathology. By unrolling proximal algorithms into neural networks, my models achieve more robust color decomposition and improved stain appearance across images.
